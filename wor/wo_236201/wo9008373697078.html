Let us cite an example of Annie, assigned as a QVA Reviewer. The Pursuit Lead schedules the review after confirming with Annie.

Let us see how Annie conducts the QVA review.

Annie starts the review with discussing the client needs and our value proposition and understanding the solution, approach, and deliverables.

She verifies whether the team is clear on the value and differentiator to win?

The team was not clear on the value and Annie discusses to make our value proposition compelling.

Annie next asks: what is our solution approach?

Annie confirms whether the approach is above or beyond the scope of what the client has asked.

In this case, it is a strategy project.

So, Annie looks at how the project is starting and if the team has all the input deliverables from the client.

Further, she asks: are the deliverables clearly defined and ownership understood by the team?

Once the approach is determined, Annie looks at the Staffing model.

Does the team have the right people, with the right skills, in the right location, at the right time, to deliver the project? What is the risk?

After validating with the team, Annie recognizes that 3 data analysts and 2 consulting people should be added.

When all those things are aligned, Annie moves to the pricing model.

Is the commercial arrangement appropriate for the nature of work?

Are we priced to win?

Annie states that every deal should have contingency.

Have we adequately assessed the delivery risk and priced in contingency accordingly?

Annie then refers the amber or red QSRM scoring and discusses risk mitigants.
